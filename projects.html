Lévy processes and its applications in derivative pricing

I researched this for my master's thesis and is available to view here.

I initially became interested in Lévy processes and exp-Lévy models as it provided an alternative to the standard Black-Scholes-Merton model, which assumes continuity at any time-scale and no jumps.

In red there is a path of the Wiener process (used in the BSM model). This is overlaid by EUR/JPY forex data from 17/01/2022 00:00 to 02:00 GMT in 1 second intervals. As we can see, the pathwise properties of the model and actual data are dissimilar, thus we need something that models jumps at random times, see Jump processes - an example of a Lévy process that is used as a starting point in my thesis. Through the use of the theory of Lévy processes and Fourier-based methods, we are then able to present an option pricing method using the exp-Lévy model.
